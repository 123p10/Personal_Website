PCHackers

In the summer of 2018 a group of Port Credit Secondary School students decided to pursue the goal of hosting "Canada's Largest High School Student run hackathon". Initially a group of 7 members grew to 25. We spent the summer searching for a venue as this was the primary concern and expense. We toured numerous buildings, college and university campuses. We eventually settled on the University of Toronto Mississauga as it provided a great venue, at a reputable location for a fairly affordable fee. So much organization went into planning this, we had to raise sponsorships, arrange for affordable and diverse food selections, arranging for guest speakers, acquiring judges from diverse backgrounds and much, much more. We were able to be officially registered under MLH(Major League Hacking), the official hackathon organization and that allowed us to get over 200 participants at the hackathon. As the logistics manager on the day of the hackathon I was in charge of making sure everything went smoothly, making sure everything was on scheduele from the food to the judging to the ceremonies. This meant that I was there at the hackathon at 7am and only left when everything was cleaned up around 2pm the next day. The over 24 hours we spent working at the hackathon was a fantastic experience that I will never forget.
