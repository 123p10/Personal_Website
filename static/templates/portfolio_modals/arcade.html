Custom Arcade Machine

2016

At the start of high school I began the construction of a custom arcade machine. Over the course of about a year I built the frame, installed the controls and display, soldered the GPIO connections and setup the operating system. The system uses the RetroPie operating system installed on a Raspberry Pi 1 Model B. The Joystick and Buttons are from Adafruit.com The display is a cheap TV screen I purchased from a local TV store.
The development of this arcade machine was incredibly difficult at the time but a fantastic experience. When I built this arcade machine in the early years of my High School, the Raspberry Pi community was not as expansive as it is today. The biggest struggle which took months to solve was that implementing multiplayer control in the RetroPie operating system was not as plug and play as it is today. I had to spend many nights messing with the source code, configuration files and GPIO setups to get the system to actually work with two joysticks and two sets of buttons at the same time.
