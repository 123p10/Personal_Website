The 6ix Robotics

In the summer of 2018 I founded the 6ix robotics team with 2 other members of 4659 robotics, due to disagreements with the school staff. We were able to recruit approximately 10 more members for the team and raised $15,000 in part from sponsorships from Deloitte, IBM and various other large companies.

On the team I was one of the head builders, the lead programmer and the main driver. Throughout the season we produced numerous bots and attended multiple Vex competitions, winning many awards.

As head programmer, I was in charge of planning the electrical system, the sensors and interfacing with them through the Vex cortex. I was also in charge of the autonomous function of the robot, a 15 second period where the robot drove autonomously. I used various linear control algorithms including PID and bang bang to perform consistent autonomous operation. We won almost every single autonomous mode we ever played.

In January of 2018 we attended the Vex provincial championships, competing against teams with $200,000+ budgets and 100s of members. We missed the world championships by one point in the provincials skills competition. Despite this, creating this team from nothing and competing as the underdogs across the province was an amazing experience.
At the end of the year, as much of the team was graduating high school, we handed the team off to the younger members of our team and they continue to compete under our name today.
